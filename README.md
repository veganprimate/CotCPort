A mod for **Octopath Traveler 0** that aims to integrate as much content as possible from **Octopath Traveler: Champions of the Continent** (Global, Japanese, and Chinese versions) and the other Octopath games too into Octopath Traveler 0.
- Play all **280+ characters** from CotC complete with **skill boards**, **voicelines**, **character art**, and **custom Wishvale integration**, including **15 fully custom playable characters** _(Nomos the Scholar, Fra, Pierro, Fried, Hugo, Captain Gimel, Mattias, Miguel, Erhardt, Kit Crossford, Zelig, Vestal of Galdera Lianna, Lucian the Glorious, Frostwalker Promme, and Vanessa Hysel)_
- Fight against hundreds of **new bosses** from CotC in the **Monster Arena**, including two **fully custom** challenge fights, one against the **Bargello Family**, the other against the **Bravely Trio**
- Unlock dozens of **new accessories** and **equipment** from CotC by beating the new enemies in the Monster Arena; the stronger the enemy, the better the accessories
- Play through dozens of **new quests** from CotC including characters' **Traveler Stories**
- Watch **hundreds** of memoirs which have been implemented as Party Banters at the memoir locations
- Experience **entirely new ailments/status effects** ported from CotC and implemented with C++ mods

Currently **work in progress (WIP) / unfinished / planned** features of the mod include but are not limited to:

- A **seamless real-time** day/night switch that works **even on Orsterra maps**. For Orsterra's night maps, I have planned **custom NPC presets** faithful to Octopath Traveler lore
- **500+** new maps reconstructed from their CotC, OT1, and OT2 counterparts, this includes **new worldmaps** for **Solistia** and **Dreadborn Orsterra**
- **OT0-ified** versions of **side-quests** from Octopath Traveler 1 and 2
- An _optional_ **hard-mode** version that significantly increases the difficulty of the **base game** as well as **added content** from **CotC, OT1, and OT2**. See [Hard Mode Version](https://github.com/veganprimate/CotCPort_Canary/blob/main/Resources/HardMode_en.md) for more info
- A **custom questline** revolving around Kit Crossford, heavily inspired by Octopath Traveler 1, including **custom boss fights**
- **Custom C++ mods** to implement larger CotC subsystems with, such as:
  - **Divine Beasts**-related battle options
  - **Latent Powers**
  - **pets**
  - custom **UI** and **gameplay** logic for **tournaments** at the Victor's Hollow Arena
  - **Grade** for weapons and accessories, **Dreadborn corridor roguelike mechanics**
  - the **Tower of Trials**
  - **new building types** such as in the **Nameless Town**
  - better **integration with Wishvale** of the new content
  - ...and more

The mod supports the following languages:
- **English**
- **Japanese**
- **Korean**
- **Mandarin Chinese** (Simplified)
- **Mandarin Chinese** (Traditional)

## Installation
Head to [Releases](https://github.com/veganprimate/CotCPort/releases) and download the archives `CotCPort_Base.7z` and `CotCPort_Extras.7z` of the latest (pre-)release. 

 - `CotCPort_Base.7z` contains the **base mod files** which **must be installed**. The DLL mods depend on each other.
 - `CotCPort_Extras.7z` is a loose collection of DLL and pak mods. **None of them are necessary**, and they **do not depend on each other**.

The archives are arranged to mirror the internal game folder:
```
OCTOPATH TRAVELER 0/
└── Octopath_Traveler0/
    ├── Binaries/
    │   └── Win64/
    └── Content/
        └── Paks/
```        
The goal is that the files from the archive should readily **merge into** the existing `Octopath_Traveler0` folder. Do not accidentally create an extra nested folder such as:
```
Octopath_Traveler0/Octopath_Traveler0/...
```
or:
```
Octopath_Traveler0/CotCPort_Base/Octopath_Traveler0/...
```
For more details on the files within both archives, see the section "Layout of the archives" in the release notes for [v1.1.0-pre](https://github.com/veganprimate/CotCPort/releases/tag/v1.1.0-pre).

Installation may require you to **replace** a number of DLLs under `Binaries/Win64/`, please also ensure `CotCPort_notex_p.pak` from v1.0.0 of the CotCPort is **removed** from `Content/Paks/` before starting the game as `CotCPort_DataBase_p.pak` is supposed replace it but due to differing filenames, won't do that on its own.

**WARNING:** As of now, there are **no Switch-compatible versions** of the **C++ mods** developed for the CotCPort nor an equivalent **native runtime layer** for installing hooks/detours/etc. The Switch version is also based on v1.0.4.0 of OCTOPATH TRAVELER 0, not v1.0.8.0 which the mods were based on.

It is **untested** whether the C++ mod loader is **compatible** with the Microsoft Store version of OT0 v1.0.8.0. The MS Store Win64 Shipping executable **may be different** to the Steam version's, thus displacing RVAs.

Nintendo Switch and MS Store players will possibly have to **constrain** themselves **to only using the paks** from the prerelease, more info below.
 
### Steam
Extract the archive(s) under `steamapps\common\OCTOPATH TRAVELER 0\Octopath_Traveler0\`.

### Microsoft Store
1. Rename the pak suffix from lowercase `_p` to uppercase `_P` for all of the paks
2. Open the **Xbox App** on your PC, right-click **OCTOPATH TRAVELER 0** in your installed games list on the left sidebar, and select **Manage**
3. Click on the **Files** tab
4. Click **Browse**
5. Extract the archive(s) under `<game install folder>\Octopath_Traveler0\`

**WARNING:** As compatibility of the C++ Modding API with the MS Store version of OT0 is still **unproven**, and if it turns out to be **negative** on your system (check if logs have been generated under `Binaries/Win64/KingshipLogs/`), please **remove `CotCPort_BluePrints_P.pak`** from your `Content/Paks/` folder as this pak is dependent on the C++ mods `CotCPort_Menus` and `CotCPort_BattleUI`. 

### Switch (real hardware/Atmosphère)
The Switch version should be treated as **pak-only**. **The Windows C++ mods do not work on Switch** and **no replacement has been made yet**.
The Switch installation uses **Atmosphère LayeredFS**. You are not replacing files inside the game install directly. Instead, you place the modded RomFS-style files on the SD card, and Atmosphère overlays them when the game starts.
The OT0 title ID is `01005270232F2000`, create this folder on the SD card:
```
sdmc:/atmosphere/contents/01005270232F2000/romfs/Octopath_Traveler0/Content/Paks/
```
place every pak **besides `CotCPort_BluePrints_p.pak`** inside:
```
sdmc:/atmosphere/contents/01005270232F2000/romfs/Octopath_Traveler0/Content/Paks/CotCPort_BluePrints_p.pak sdmc:/atmosphere/contents/01005270232F2000/romfs/Octopath_Traveler0/Content/Paks/CotCPort_Maps_p.pak sdmc:/atmosphere/contents/01005270232F2000/romfs/Octopath_Traveler0/Content/Paks/CotCPort_Sounds_p.pak sdmc:/atmosphere/contents/01005270232F2000/romfs/Octopath_Traveler0/Content/Paks/CotCPort_Textures_p.pak
```
All the paks in `CotCPort_Extras.7z` are compatible as well.

### Switch (Emulator)
Analogous to the real hardware, this platform should also be treated as **pak-only** for the same reason.
Create a mod with the following **internal** layout: 
```
romfs/
└── Octopath_Traveler0/
    └── Content/
        └── Paks/
             ├── CotCPort_DataBase_p.pak
             ├── CotCPort_Maps_p.pak
             ├── CotCPort_Sounds_p.pak
             ├── CotCPort_Textures_p.pak
             └── (+ any of the pak mods in CotCPort_Extras.7z)
```

### Other platforms
Beyond the platforms mentioned here, OCTOPATH TRAVELER 0 is also available on PS4, PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and Switch 2. **None** of these platforms have viable or consistent methods for installing even pak mods.

## How to recruit the new travelers
Characters' **traveler stories** currently serve as their recruitment events, however, that only covers 140 out of the 280+ newly added characters. I am planning on adding **unique recruitment events** for each and every new playable character in this mod but that will still take some time.

For now, you can use the **optional** pak mod `RecruitEvent_p.pak` in the `CotCPort_Extras.7z` archive to recruit all character this mod provides at once. 
For that, please head to **Wishvale Trail** and speak with the **Forgetful Squirrel**, pictured below:

<img width="1239" height="541" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a39b0888-f820-462b-81c0-1a747ab59528" />

A prompt with **four options** will pop up:
 1. Recruit all mod characters + Add them to Wishvale
 2. Recruit all mod characters
 3. Unrecruit all mod characters
 4. Remove all mod characters from Wishvale

When an update to the mod introduces new playable characters, you can recruit them by **talking to the same NPC again** provided `RecruitEvent_p.pak` is in your paks folder.
The visible add-member effect is **suppressed** for already recruited characters because `EntryPlayerCharacter` returns `false` for those, and opcode `10029  EvCmAddPartyMember` does not force the dialog, but that does not mean `10029` is skipped for already recruited characters. It is just that what it does is neglegible: an already recruited player may still be processed, have their HP/SP healed, position them in the party, refresh active party cue sheet, etc. They will **not** lose their equipment or equipped skills, have their stats reset, or be recruited twice.

**WARNING 1**: Be careful about recruiting characters too early now that the recruit event is at **Wishvale Trail**. During the prologue, all of your party members have their **equipment slots locked**. Any character that is recruited during this quest-state also has their equipment slots locked. This effect is **not undone** by completing the prologue, you have to **unrecruit and re-recruit** all the affected characters

**WARNING 2:** The stats of the recruited characters are scaled according to **whoever interacts with the squirrel**, i.e. depending on who the party leader is (i.e. in the upper left corner in the formation menu)
